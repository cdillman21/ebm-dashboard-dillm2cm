# Stakeholder Evidence: Methods & Engagement Strategy
# Document your approach to gathering stakeholder perspectives and feedback

## Stakeholder Evidence Goals
**Primary Goal:** Understand early-career employee perspectives on compensation satisfaction, career development opportunities, and manager effectiveness to identify specific retention drivers and pain points from those directly affected.

**Secondary Goal:** Gather feedback from managers, HR partners, and senior leadership on feasibility, concerns, and support for proposed retention solutions (compensation adjustments, development programs, manager training).

## Target Stakeholder Groups

### Internal Stakeholders
#### Management/Leadership
- **Target Participants:** VP-level and above (C-suite, functional VPs), Department Heads managing early-career employees
- **Sample Size Target:** 15-20 leaders
- **Engagement Method:** One-on-one interviews (30 minutes) or small group discussions (5-6 leaders, 60 minutes)
- **Key Questions Focus:** 
  - What retention challenges do you see in your teams?
  - What budget/resources are available for retention solutions?
  - What concerns do you have about compensation adjustments or new programs?
  - How would you measure success of retention initiatives?
- **Expected Response Rate:** 75-85% (high priority topic, leadership invested)

#### Employees/Staff - Early-Career Cohort (PRIMARY STAKEHOLDER GROUP)
- **Target Participants:** Employees with 0-3 years tenure in professional/technical roles
- **Sample Size Target:** 100-150 survey responses, 15-20 interviews
- **Engagement Method:** Anonymous online survey + voluntary follow-up interviews
- **Key Questions Focus:** 
  - What factors most influence your decision to stay or leave?
  - How satisfied are you with your compensation package?
  - Are you receiving adequate career development and feedback?
  - How effective is your manager in supporting your growth?
  - What would make you more likely to stay long-term?
- **Expected Response Rate:** 40-50% for survey, 15-20% for interviews (offering incentive)

#### Managers of Early-Career Employees
- **Target Participants:** First-line and mid-level managers with direct reports in 0-3 year tenure range
- **Sample Size Target:** 40-50 managers
- **Engagement Method:** Online survey + focus groups (6-8 managers per session)
- **Key Questions Focus:** 
  - What challenges do you face in retaining your early-career team members?
  - Do you have the tools/training to support career development conversations?
  - What feedback do you receive from your team about compensation, growth opportunities?
  - What support do you need to improve retention on your team?
- **Expected Response Rate:** 60-70% (directly relevant to their job performance)

#### HR Business Partners
- **Target Participants:** HRBPs supporting departments with high early-career populations
- **Sample Size Target:** 8-10 HRBPs
- **Engagement Method:** Structured interviews (45 minutes each)
- **Key Questions Focus:**
  - What retention themes are you hearing from employees and managers?
  - What solutions have worked or failed in your client groups?
  - What organizational barriers exist to implementing retention programs?
- **Expected Response Rate:** 90-100% (part of their role)

### External Stakeholders
#### Recent Departures (Exit Cohort)
- **Target Participants:** Employees who voluntarily left within last 12 months with 0-3 years tenure
- **Sample Size Target:** 30-40 individuals
- **Engagement Method:** Online exit survey (if not already completed) + optional follow-up call
- **Key Questions Focus:** 
  - What was the primary reason for your departure?
  - What could the organization have done to retain you?
  - How does your new role/compensation compare?
  - Would you consider returning in the future?
- **Expected Response Rate:** 25-35% (lower - no longer employed, less incentive)

## Data Collection Methods

### Survey Design
#### Online Survey Platform
- **Platform Choice:** Qualtrics (enterprise license available through organization)
- **Survey Length:** 10-15 minutes (25-30 questions total)
- **Question Types:** 60% Likert scales (1-5), 30% multiple choice/ranking, 10% open-ended
- **Incentives:** 
  - Current employees: Entry into raffle for $200 gift cards (5 winners)
  - Former employees: $25 gift card for completion
  - Managers: No incentive needed (job-related)

#### Key Survey Questions
**Problem Assessment Questions (Early-Career Employees):**
- "How satisfied are you with your current total compensation package?" (1-5 scale)
- "How clearly do you understand your career path and advancement opportunities?" (1-5 scale)
- "My manager actively supports my professional development" (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
- "In the past 6 months, have you seriously considered leaving the organization?" (Yes/No + reasons)
- "What factors would most influence your decision to stay long-term?" (Rank top 3: compensation, growth, manager,
 culture, work-life balance, mission, benefits)

**Problem Assessment Questions (Managers):**
- "What challenges do you face in retaining early-career employees on your team?" (Open-ended)
- "How adequate are the tools and resources available to support team member development?" (1-5 scale)
- "What percentage of your early-career employees have left voluntarily in the past 12 months?" (0%, <10%, 10-20%, 
20-30%, >30%)

**Solution Feedback Questions (All Stakeholders):**
- "How important is competitive compensation for retaining early-career talent?" (Not important to Extremely 
important)
- "How feasible is implementing a formal mentorship program?" (Not feasible to Very feasible)
- "What concerns do you have about compensation adjustments for early-career roles?" (Select all: budget, internal 
equity, sustainability, fairness, other)
- "If manager training on career development were offered, would you participate/support it?" (Definitely yes to
 Definitely no)

**Priority and Support Questions:**
- "Compared to other organizational priorities, how important is improving early-career retention?" (Not a priority
 to Top priority)
- "What level of resources should be allocated to retention initiatives?" (Minimal to Significant)
- "I would actively support changes to improve early-career retention" (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

### Interview Protocol
#### Interview Structure
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes per interview
- **Format:** Video conference (Zoom/Teams) with option for phone if preferred
- **Recording:** Audio recording with participant consent; deleted after transcription
- **Note-taking:** Real-time notes in structured template + AI transcription for backup

#### Interview Questions

**Opening (All Stakeholders):**
- Can you describe your role and how you interact with early-career employees?
- How long have you been in your current position?

**For Early-Career Employees:**
- What attracted you to join this organization initially?
- How has your experience here matched (or not matched) your expectations?
- Can you describe your relationship with your manager and how they support your growth?
- What would make you more likely to see yourself here in 3-5 years?
- If you were to leave, what would be the primary reason?
- What advice would you give leadership about retaining people at your career stage?

**For Managers:**
- What trends have you noticed in early-career employee retention on your team?
- What makes retention challenging for this population specifically?
- How confident do you feel in your ability to develop and retain early-career talent?
- What support or resources would help you be more effective in retention?
- Have you seen any retention strategies work well? Any that failed?

**For HR Business Partners:**
- What themes emerge from exit interviews with early-career employees?
- How does early-career retention vary across different departments?
- What organizational barriers exist to improving retention?
- What solutions have been tried before, and what were the results?

**For Senior Leadership:**
- How does early-career turnover impact your strategic objectives?
- What trade-offs would you be willing to make to improve retention?
- What concerns do you have about proposed solutions (comp adjustments, training programs)?
- How should we measure ROI on retention investments?

### Focus Group Protocol
#### Structure
- **Group Size:** 6-8 participants per session
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Number of Sessions:** 3-4 (segmented by stakeholder group: early-career employees, frontline managers,
 mid-level leaders, mixed)
- **Facilitation:** External facilitator for candor; HR observer for notes only

#### Discussion Guide
**Topic 1: Problem Definition (20 minutes)**
- "In your experience, is early-career retention a problem here? Why or why not?"
- "What makes early-career employees leave versus stay?"
- Group exercise: Prioritize retention factors on sticky notes

**Topic 2: Current State Analysis (25 minutes)**
- "What does the organization currently do well for early-career development?"
- "Where are the gaps or missed opportunities?"
- "How does manager quality vary, and what's the impact?"

**Topic 3: Solution Brainstorming (30 minutes)**
- "If you could change one thing to improve retention, what would it be?"
- "React to these potential solutions: [present 3-4 evidence-based options]"
- "What would make these solutions work (or fail) in our context?"

**Topic 4: Implementation Realities (15 minutes)**
- "What obstacles would we face in implementing changes?"
- "Who needs to be involved for success?"
- "What timeline is realistic?"

## Access Strategy

### Internal Permissions
**Approvals Needed:**
1. CHRO approval for employee surveys and manager interviews
2. Legal review of survey questions (privacy/compliance)
3. Department head approval for focus group participation during work hours
4. Communications team approval for survey invitation messaging

**Timeline for Approvals:** 2-3 weeks advance notice

### Recruitment Strategy

**For Surveys:**
- Email invitation from CHRO with project explanation
- Follow-up reminders at Day 5 and Day 10
- Anonymous link (no tracking of who responded)
- 2-week response window

**For Interviews:**
- Solicitation email with volunteer sign-up link
- Purposive sampling to ensure diversity (department, tenure, performance level, demographics)
- Confirmation email with calendar invite and consent form

**For Focus Groups:**
- Manager nominates willing participants OR open call for volunteers
- Ensure mix of high performers, solid contributors, varied tenures (0-3 years)
- Schedule during work hours to show organizational commitment

### Engagement Safeguards
**Confidentiality:**
- Survey responses anonymous (no IP tracking)
- Interview quotes attributed only by role type (e.g., "Early-career employee in Engineering")
- Focus group participants sign confidentiality agreement
- No individual data shared with managers or leadership - only aggregated themes

**Informed Consent:**
- Clear explanation of project purpose: "To improve early-career retention through evidence-based solutions"
- Voluntary participation emphasized
- Right to skip questions or withdraw
- Data use: Internal analysis only, findings shared in aggregate

**Follow-up Communication:**
- Share high-level findings with all participants within 30 days
- "You said, we heard" summary document
- Transparency about which recommendations will be pursued and why

## Timeline and Logistics

### Data Collection Schedule
- **Survey Launch:** Week 1 Monday (kick-off email from leadership)
- **Survey Close:** Week 3 Friday (2-week window)
- **Interview Period:** Weeks 2-5 (concurrent with survey, extends slightly beyond)
- **Focus Group Dates:** Weeks 4-6 (after initial survey data reviewed to inform discussions)

### Participation Targets
- **Early-Career Employee Survey:** Target 100 responses (50% of 0-3 year population)
- **Manager Survey:** Target 40 responses (80% of managers with early-career reports)
- **Interviews:** 15-20 total (8-10 early-career, 4-5 managers, 3-4 HR/leaders)
- **Focus Groups:** 25-30 total participants across 3-4 sessions

### Response Rate Contingency Plans
**If early-career survey response <30% by Day 10:**
- Manager encouragement (without pressure) in team meetings
- Extend deadline by 3 days
- Add lunch-and-learn incentive (pizza lunch for teams with >60% participation)

**If interview volunteers insufficient:**
- Expand eligibility criteria (include 4-year tenure employees)
- Offer flexible scheduling (early morning, evening options)
- Increase incentive (add recognition/LinkedIn recommendation from CHRO)

### Alternative Methods
**If survey fatigue is high:**
- Shorten survey to 5-7 critical questions only
- Add pulse survey approach (2-3 questions monthly over 3 months)

**If formal interviews are challenging:**
- Conduct informal coffee chats (15-20 minutes, less formal)
- Leverage existing one-on-one meeting time with HR BP facilitation

## Ethical Considerations

### Informed Consent
- **Purpose Explanation:** "We're studying early-career retention to design evidence-based solutions. Your input
 will shape compensation, development, and manager training initiatives."
- **Participation Voluntariness:** "Participation is completely voluntary. Choosing not to participate will not 
affect your employment, performance reviews, or opportunities in any way."
- **Data Use:** "Responses will be aggregated and analyzed by the HR Strategy team. No individual responses will 
be shared with your manager or leadership. Findings will inform organizational retention programs."
- **Anonymity/Confidentiality:** 
  - Surveys: Fully anonymous
  - Interviews: Confidential (names known to researcher but quotes de-identified in reports)
  - Focus groups: Confidential within group (participants agree not to share others' comments)

### Organizational Permissions
- **Supervisor Approval:** Not required for survey participation (voluntary during personal time or work time)
- **HR Consultation:** HR leadership is project sponsor - full coordination throughout
- **Ethics Review:** Submit to internal review board (or equivalent) for approval before launch
- **Union Notification:** If applicable, notify union representatives and offer to include union perspective in 
interviews

### Participant Protections
- **No Retaliation:** Explicit statement that candid feedback (even negative) will not result in negative 
consequences
- **Data Security:** Survey data stored on encrypted, password-protected servers; interview recordings deleted
 after transcription
- **Reporting Safeguards:** Minimum cell size of n=10 for any subgroup reporting (e.g., won't report "Sales team 
satisfaction" if only 8 sales employees)

## Data Management Plan

### Data Collection Organization
- **Response Tracking:** Qualtrics tracks completion rate (not individual identities); Interview schedule 
spreadsheet tracks confirmed/completed
- **Data Storage:** 
  - Survey data: Qualtrics secure server, exported to password-protected Excel
  - Interview recordings: Encrypted cloud storage (Box), access limited to research team
  - Transcripts: De-identified and stored separately from audio
- **Data Security:** Two-factor authentication, role-based access control, automatic backup daily
- **Backup Plan:** Weekly exports to secure local drive; redundant cloud storage

### Analysis Preparation
- **Coding Strategy:** 
  - Open-ended survey responses: Thematic coding using NVivo or manual Excel coding
  - Interview transcripts: Deductive coding based on logic model (X, M, Y factors) + inductive emergent themes
  - Code book developed after reviewing 20% of data, refined iteratively
  
- **Quantitative Analysis:** 
  - Descriptive statistics (means, frequencies, distributions) for all Likert scales
  - Cross-tabulations by tenure, department, performance level
  - Correlation analysis (satisfaction metrics vs. retention intent)
  - Statistical significance testing where sample size permits (t-tests, chi-square)
  
- **Integration Plan:** 
  - Triangulate survey quantitative results with qualitative interview themes
  - Use focus group discussions to validate/refine survey findings
  - Create evidence matrix mapping themes across methods (survey + interviews + focus groups)
  - Weight evidence by source credibility and consistency

### Analysis Timeline
- **Week 4-5:** Preliminary survey data analysis (descriptive stats, frequency distributions)
- **Week 6-7:** Interview transcription and initial coding
- **Week 7-8:** Focus group data synthesis
- **Week 8-9:** Integrated analysis and evidence synthesis
- **Week 10:** Stakeholder report and recommendations development
