doesn't, and critical implementation factors.

**Secondary Goal:** Understand practical constraints, common pitfalls, and realistic timelines for implementing 
compensation adjustments and manager development programs based on practitioner experience.

## Acquisition Methods

### Expert Interviews
#### Interview Planning
- **Target Number:** 5-7 interviews (mix of primary interviews + secondary source analysis)
- **Interview Length:** 45-60 minutes per interview
- **Interview Method:** Video conference (Zoom/Teams) for remote; in-person for local practitioners
- **Recording Plan:** 
  - Request permission to audio record for transcription
  - If declined, detailed notes taken during interview + summary sent for validation
  - All recordings deleted after transcription and validation

#### Interview Protocol

**Opening Questions:**
- Can you tell me about your role and experience with employee retention, particularly for early-career employees?
- How long have you been working in talent management/HR, and what types of organizations have you worked with?
- Have you personally led or been involved in early-career retention initiatives?

**Problem-Focused Questions:**
- Have you encountered high turnover among early-career employees (0-3 years tenure) in your work? 
How common is this problem?
- What do you see as the primary drivers of early-career turnover? (Prompt: compensation, development,
 manager quality, culture, work-life balance)
- How does early-career turnover differ from turnover among more tenured employees?
- What are the "red flags" or early warning signs that an organization has an early-career retention problem?
- In your experience, what makes this problem particularly challenging to solve?

**Solution-Focused Questions:**
- What compensation strategies have you seen work effectively for early-career retention?
  - Prompt: Base salary vs. total rewards approach? Equity? Student loan benefits?
- How important is pay competitiveness versus pay growth trajectory?
- Beyond compensation, what other factors are critical for retaining early-career talent?
- What role does manager quality or manager training play in retention?
- Can you share an example of a successful retention intervention you've implemented or observed?
- What approaches have you tried that didn't work as expected? Why did they fail?

**Implementation Questions:**
- What are the biggest obstacles to implementing compensation adjustments for early-career employees?
  - Prompt: Budget constraints? Internal equity concerns? Leadership buy-in?
- How do you balance market competitiveness with internal pay equity?
- For manager training/development programs, what are the critical success factors?
- How long does it typically take to see results from retention initiatives?
- What metrics do you use to measure success of retention programs?

**Context-Specific Questions:**
- Does company size (startup vs. large enterprise) affect which retention strategies work best?
- Are there industry differences in what early-career employees value most?
- How has the labor market changed post-pandemic in terms of early-career retention?

**Closing Questions:**
- If you had to prioritize one intervention for early-career retention, what would it be and why?
- What advice would you give to an organization just starting to address this problem?
- Are there any resources, tools, or research you'd recommend I look into?
- Is there anything else about early-career retention that I haven't asked about but should know?
- May I follow up with you if clarifying questions arise?
- What approaches have you seen work for this type of problem?
- What solutions have you tried or observed?
- What would you recommend based on your experience?

**Implementation Questions:**
- What challenges should we expect when implementing this type of solution?
- What would you do differently if you were addressing this problem again?
- What resources or support are critical for success?

**Closing Questions:**
- Who else would you recommend I speak with about this?
- Is there anything important I haven't asked about?
