# Practitioner Evidence: How I Found HR Experts to Talk To
# My approach to getting insights from people who actually do this work

## What I Wanted to Learn From Practitioners
**Main Goal:** Get real-world advice from HR leaders about what actually works for keeping early-career employees, 
what doesn't work, and what I need to watch out for when implementing solutions.

**Bonus Goal:** Understand the practical stuff - like budget constraints, common mistakes, and realistic timelines 
for rolling out compensation changes and manager training.

## Who I Wanted to Talk To

### The Ideal Experts
**Profile I Was Looking For:** Senior HR professionals who've actually dealt with retention and compensation issues
- **Experience:** 10+ years in HR/talent management, with at least 5 years in leadership (so they've seen enough to know what works)
- **Industry:** Tech, professional services, consulting, or similar knowledge-work companies (where I want to work)
- **Roles I Targeted:** 
  - Chief People Officers (CPOs) or VPs of HR
  - Heads of Talent Management or Compensation
  - HR Business Partners working with early-career employees
  - Compensation & Benefits Directors
- **Company Size:** Mid-size to large (500+ employees) with real early-career programs
- **Location:** Mostly U.S.-based (where I'll be working)

## How I Actually Got the Information

### Interviews with Experts
#### My Interview Plan
- **Target:** 5-7 total (mix of actual interviews + researching published sources)
- **Length:** 45-60 minutes each
- **Method:** Zoom/Teams for remote people; in-person for local ones
- **Recording:** 
  - Asked permission to record for transcription
  - If they said no, took detailed notes + sent summary for them to check
  - Deleted all recordings after transcribing

#### Questions I Asked

**Getting Started:**
- Can you tell me about your role and experience with retention, especially for early-career employees?
- How long have you been in HR, and what types of companies have you worked at?
- Have you personally led retention initiatives for early-career folks?

**About the Problem:**
- Have you seen high turnover among early-career employees (0-3 years)? How common is this?
- What do YOU think are the main reasons early-career people quit? (I prompted: pay, development, managers, culture, work-life balance)
- How is early-career turnover different from experienced employee turnover?
- What are the "red flags" that tell you a company has an early-career retention problem?
- What makes this problem hard to solve?

**About Solutions:**
- What compensation strategies have actually worked for keeping early-career employees?
  - Asked specifically: Base salary vs. total package? Stock options? Student loan help?
- How important is paying competitively vs. giving good raises over time?
- Besides money, what else really matters for keeping early-career talent?
- How much does having a good manager or training managers matter?
- Can you give me an example of a retention program that really worked?
- What have you tried that DIDN'T work? Why did it fail?

**About Implementation:**
- What are the biggest roadblocks to adjusting pay for early-career employees?
  - Prompted: Budget? Internal fairness issues? Getting leadership buy-in?
- How do you balance market rates with internal pay equity?
- For manager training/development programs, what are the critical success factors?
- How long does it typically take to see results from retention initiatives?
- What metrics do you use to measure success of retention programs?

**Context-Specific Questions:**
- Does company size (startup vs. large enterprise) affect which retention strategies work best?
- Are there industry differences in what early-career employees value most?
- How has the labor market changed post-pandemic in terms of early-career retention?

**Closing Questions:**
- If you had to prioritize one intervention for early-career retention, what would it be and why?
- What advice would you give to an organization just starting to address this problem?
- Are there any resources, tools, or research you'd recommend I look into?
- Is there anything else about early-career retention that I haven't asked about but should know?
- May I follow up with you if clarifying questions arise?
- What approaches have you seen work for this type of problem?
- What solutions have you tried or observed?
- What would you recommend based on your experience?

**Implementation Questions:**
- What challenges should we expect when implementing this type of solution?
- What would you do differently if you were addressing this problem again?
- What resources or support are critical for success?

**Closing Questions:**
- Who else would you recommend I speak with about this?
- Is there anything important I haven't asked about?
