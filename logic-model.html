Logic Model: Employee Compensation & Talent Retention

Evidence-Based Management Approach to Early Career Professional Retention

Problem Statement

Current Challenge: Organizations struggle to retain high-performing early career professionals, leading to increased turnover costs, knowledge loss, and reduced competitive advantage. Traditional compensation packages may not align with what early career professionals value most.

Inputs/Resources

  • HR budget for compensation analysis
  • Employee survey data
  • Scientific research literature
  • Industry benchmarking data
  • Exit interview feedback
  • Management time and expertise
  • Data analytics tools
  • External consultants (if needed)
  • Technology platforms
→

Activities

  • Conduct systematic literature review
  • Survey early career employees
  • Analyze current compensation packages
  • Benchmark against industry standards
  • Interview high-performing employees
  • Analyze turnover data patterns
  • Design evidence-based compensation model
  • Pilot test new approaches
  • Train managers on retention strategies
→

Outputs

  • Comprehensive research report
  • Evidence-based compensation framework
  • Employee preference analysis
  • Revised compensation packages
  • Manager training materials
  • Implementation guidelines
  • Measurement dashboard
  • Policy recommendations
  • Best practices documentation

Short-term Outcomes (0-12 months)

  • Increased employee satisfaction with compensation
  • Improved understanding of employee preferences
  • Enhanced manager capability in retention
  • More competitive compensation packages
  • Better alignment of rewards with values
  • Reduced intention to leave
  • Improved employee engagement scores
  • Enhanced employer brand perception
→

Long-term Outcomes (1-3 years)

  • Reduced turnover rates
  • Lower recruitment and training costs
  • Improved talent retention
  • Enhanced organizational knowledge retention
  • Stronger pipeline of experienced professionals
  • Increased productivity and performance
  • Better succession planning
  • Improved organizational culture
→

Ultimate Impact (3+ years)

  • Sustainable competitive advantage
  • Organizational excellence and growth
  • Industry leadership in talent management
  • Improved financial performance
  • Enhanced stakeholder value
  • Reputation as employer of choice
  • Innovation and market leadership
  • Long-term organizational sustainability

Key Assumptions

  • Early career professionals have different compensation preferences than experienced workers
  • Evidence-based approaches will be more effective than intuition-based decisions
  • Organizations have the capability to implement recommended changes
  • Compensation is a significant factor in retention decisions
  • Employees will provide honest feedback about their preferences
  • Management will support evidence-based compensation changes
  • Market conditions will remain relatively stable during implementation

External Factors (Outside Our Control)

  • Economic conditions and market volatility
  • Industry-wide talent shortages
  • Competitor compensation strategies
  • Generational workplace preference shifts
  • Regulatory changes affecting compensation
  • Technology disruption in the industry
  • Geographic talent mobility trends
  • Changes in employee expectations post-pandemic
